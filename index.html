about me

The first thing you should know about me is that I hate writing about myself; I get mad at the screen in front of me and my writing usually turns into ranting. I apologize in advance for briefly throwing you into the trenches of my inner war. The second thing about me is that I'm an autistic trans girl and therefore innately destined to be a programmer. God, I wish that was true. I do sincerely love programming and I'm getting better at it every day, but what I really am at heart is a writer. Not that I'm innately good at writing either ‚Äî if I ever believed that, I'd face eternal writer's block ‚Äî no, I'm a writer in that I see stories everywhere: in songs, in people, behind every forgettable object, every menial tasks; I see stories in code. The path of logic the computer travels, it's like a narrative to me, a hero's journey, and writing that code is just as much of a story too. When you're finished with a project, you have what all stories are: an expression. An expression of self, of intention, of action. This is a perspective that's hard to describe; it doesn't make much sense to me either, but what makes more sense is what drives it. I've felt excluded and outcasted from the world for much of my life and so I have a deep need to feel like my presence and existence impact it in some tangible way. The most fulfilling way I've found to achieve that is by making something. Taking something from within myself and seeing it manifest outside of myself. Making it real, and therefore, making myself real. If the fabric of the universe is woven out of stories, adding more stories is the way to become part of it. And that's why I'm trying to become what you would call a programmer. I'm an autistic writer who thinks the language of computers is just as beautiful if not more so than the language of books. Thank you for your patience and for indulging me.

experience

Experience is a tricky thing to talk about, especially for someone in my place in life. I could write out a list; it would be relatively short. As you might be able to tell from my repositories below, I'm just now learning how to program for the first time in my life. I could talk about my six months of food service industry work at a donut shop in late highschool where I learned how to fit into a workplace. I could talk about my time as an anthropology major where, last year (2022), I hit my first major dead end and learned how to face it, but I have so many more valuable experiences that don't fit into the typical resume perception of the word.
